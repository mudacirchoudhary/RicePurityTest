What is the Rice Purity Test?

The Rice Purity Test is designed to promote self-awareness in a fun way. People take this test to get an idea about their life choices by analyzing different life categories including behavior, relationship, habits, lifestyle choice, social behavior, cultural relativism through a questionnaire.

The test originated at Rice University by students to check their innocence and life experiences in a quiz format. When you start your test, a list of 100 questions is given to you. You have to tick one box and leave the other option empty.

100 Questions

Seems like tough, in fact it is not, since these 100 questions are short statements. The test is completely anonymous and can be taken online. It is based on 100 yes-or-no questions, each question covers different aspects of life. Questions reflect different situations, in which you have to agree with or disagree by selecting the appropriate options. A 'yes' shows you experienced that particular situation whereas no reflects no experiences.

How does the Rice Purity Test work?

You have to open the site and click on "Start Test". Once done, a list of 100 questions appears on your screen. Every statement follows "Yes" or "No" choices.

When you select the yes option, it automatically deducts one score out of 100. If you had selected the "No" option in most of your answers, this shows you have fewer life experiences and consequently your innocence scores will be higher. However, the purpose behind this test is to test your overall experiences, it does not target your identity.

How Scoring is Calculated?

This personality test is not about just innocence, it reflects your interaction with others and your life choices. If you want appropriate self-analysis, then you need to give accurate answers. Every 'Yes' answer results in deduction of one point. As the number of "Yes" answers increases, your purity score decreases. You can judge your own progress instantly after completing the test.

Understanding Rice Purity Score

The final score determines where you stand on the "Innocence spectrum". For instance:

90–100 Very limited life experiences — high purity
70–89 Moderate experiences — clear demarcation
50–69 Balanced overview of personal life events
30–49 Considerable life exposure and more experiences
Below 30 Broader life incidents and experiences
Note: All the above mentioned numbers are not scientifically proven, they are just analyzed due to the test's nature. If you have the lowest innocence score, it never means that you are not pure — it reflects that you have more interaction with society, life experiences and people.
